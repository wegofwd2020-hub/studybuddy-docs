# StudyBuddy OnDemand — Marketing Strategy, Plan & Cost

A full go-to-market strategy covering brand positioning, channels, campaigns, budgets, and KPIs across three growth stages aligned with the hosting tiers in `COST_PLAN.md`.

---

## 1. Positioning & Messaging

### Core Value Proposition

> **Instant AI tutoring for every student — at the price of a Netflix subscription.**

No wait time. No API key. Works offline. Safe for under-13s. In English, French, and Spanish.

### Positioning by Audience

| Audience | Primary Message | Fear Being Addressed |
|---|---|---|
| **Parent (B2C)** | "Your child gets a personal STEM tutor for $8/month — less than one tutoring hour" | My child is falling behind and I can't afford a tutor |
| **Student (B2C, 13+)** | "Ace your STEM exams with lessons written for your exact grade, available at 2am" | Exams are tomorrow and I don't understand the topic |
| **Teacher (B2B entry)** | "Upload your syllabus. AI generates lessons, quizzes, and audio for your students within hours" | I spend hours creating resources my students barely use |
| **School Admin (B2B close)** | "COPPA/FERPA-compliant AI tutoring your district can actually approve" | Our IT/legal team blocks every AI tool |
| **District Buyer (B2B enterprise)** | "Measurable learning outcomes across every school in your district — at <$8/student/month" | How do I justify AI EdTech spend to the board? |

### Tone of Voice

- **Confident, not arrogant.** The product works — say so plainly.
- **Parent-friendly, not academic.** Avoid jargon. "Your child" not "the learner."
- **Evidence-first.** Lead with numbers: cost, grade levels covered, response time, pass rates.
- **Safety-forward.** COPPA compliance and no-data-sharing messaging is a differentiator, not a footnote.

## 2. Target Audiences

### B2C Personas

**Persona 1 — The Stretched Parent**
- Demographics: 35–50, household income $50K–$120K, 1–3 school-age children
- Geography: Canada, USA, UK, Australia (English-first launch); France, Francophone Africa (Phase 2)
- Behaviour: Searches "grade 8 math help", "science tutor near me", spends on Duolingo, Khan Academy
- Decision driver: Cost vs. private tutor ($60–$120/hr); child's next exam is the trigger
- Channel: Google Search, Facebook/Instagram parenting groups, school newsletter ads

**Persona 2 — The Self-Directed Student (13–18)**
- Demographics: High school students, STEM-curious or exam-anxious
- Behaviour: YouTube tutorials, Reddit r/learnmath, Discord study servers, TikTok study content
- Decision driver: "Does this actually explain it better than my teacher?"
- Channel: TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Reddit, Discord study communities

**Persona 3 — The Homeschool Parent**
- Demographics: 30–45, highly engaged, price-sensitive but quality-focused
- Behaviour: Active in homeschool Facebook groups, buys curriculum bundles, values offline capability
- Decision driver: Structured STEM curriculum they don't have to build themselves
- Channel: Facebook groups (Secular Homeschool, Classical Conversations alternatives), Pinterest, homeschool co-op networks

### B2B Personas

**Persona 4 — The Champion Teacher**
- Role: Individual STEM teacher, Grade 6–12
- Behaviour: Active on Teachers Pay Teachers, follows EdTech Twitter/LinkedIn, attends local PD days
- Decision driver: "This saves me 3 hours of prep per week" — personal productivity win before institutional buy
- Channel: LinkedIn, Teachers Pay Teachers partnership, EdTech newsletters (EdSurge, MindShift)

**Persona 5 — The School Technology Coordinator**
- Role: IT Director or Ed Tech Coordinator at a private/international school
- Behaviour: Attends ISTE, BETT, local school tech conferences; evaluates tools for COPPA/FERPA compliance
- Decision driver: Compliance + ease of deployment + measurable outcomes
- Channel: LinkedIn, conference exhibit hall, school district RFP mailing lists

**Persona 6 — The Tutoring Centre Owner**
- Role: Owner/operator of a private tutoring centre (Sylvan, Kumon-style independents)
- Behaviour: Looks for digital tools to supplement in-person tutoring; wants branded student experience
- Decision driver: Reduces cost of creating materials; can white-label content
- Channel: LinkedIn, tutoring industry associations, direct outreach

## 3. Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Weakness to Exploit |
|---|---|
| **Khan Academy** | No AI personalisation; no school curriculum upload; US-centric |
| **Quizlet** | Study cards only; no lessons, no audio, no experiments; no school reporting |
| **Duolingo (STEM)** | Language-only; no STEM depth; no school contracts |
| **Tutor.com / Chegg** | $30–60/session; not scalable; no offline; no school integration |
| **ChatGPT / Claude direct** | Banned on most school networks; not COPPA compliant; requires prompt crafting |
| **Google Classroom** | LMS only; no AI content; teacher must still create everything |

**Our headline comparison line:**
> *"Khan Academy covers the basics. ChatGPT isn't allowed at school. We're the thing in between."*

## 4. Marketing Channels

### 4.1 Search Engine Optimisation (SEO)

**Strategy:** Own the long-tail search queries parents and students type the night before an exam.

Target keyword clusters:

| Cluster | Example Keywords | Monthly Search Volume (est.) |
|---|---|---|
| Grade + subject + help | "grade 8 math help", "grade 10 chemistry explained" | High (50K–200K/cluster) |
| STEM tutor alternatives | "affordable STEM tutor", "online science tutor Canada" | Medium (10K–50K) |
| Curriculum-specific | "Ontario Grade 9 science curriculum", "GCSE Physics revision" | Medium |
| Homeschool | "STEM homeschool curriculum Grade 7", "AI homeschool tutor" | Medium |
| Compliance | "COPPA compliant AI tutor", "FERPA safe EdTech" | Low (but B2B gold) |

**Content plan:** Publish 2 blog posts/week targeting these clusters. Each post follows the pattern: explain a hard Grade 8–10 STEM topic clearly, then soft-pitch the app. Example: *"How to Explain the Pythagorean Theorem to a Grade 8 Student (+ Free Practice Quiz)"*.

**Timeline to impact:** 3–6 months for organic traffic. Start immediately.

**Cost:** $0 if written in-house; $500–1,500/month if outsourced to education content writers.

---

### 4.2 Paid Search (Google Ads)

**Strategy:** Capture high-intent searchers ready to act — "online STEM tutor" and "math help for grade 9."

| Campaign Type | Target Keywords | Bid Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| B2C brand | "AI tutor grade 8", "STEM app for students" | Max conversions |
| B2C competitor | "Khan Academy alternative", "Chegg alternative" | Target CPA |
| B2B intent | "school AI curriculum tool", "FERPA compliant EdTech" | Target CPA, lower volume |

**Estimated CPCs:**
- Consumer education keywords: $0.80–$2.50 per click
- B2B/school keywords: $3–$8 per click

**Conversion funnel:** Click → Free tier signup (2 free lessons) → Paywall hit → Subscribe.

**Target CPA (cost per paying subscriber):** $15–$25 consumer; $80–$150 school lead.

### 4.3 Social Media — Organic

**TikTok / Instagram Reels (B2C, students + parents)**

Content themes:
- *"I don't understand [topic] — let me show you in 60 seconds"* (hooks students)
- *"My kid's tutor costs $0/month — here's how"* (hooks parents)
- *"POV: it's 11pm and your exam is tomorrow"* (relatable student moment)
- Behind-the-scenes: how AI generates a lesson

Target: 3 posts/week. Use trending audio. Tag #StudyTok, #STEMTutor, #GradeHelp.

**LinkedIn (B2B, teachers + admins)**

Content themes:
- Teacher productivity wins: "Our Grade 8 science teacher reduced prep time by 4 hours/week"
- Data/outcomes: pass rate improvements, struggle-unit identification
- Thought leadership: "Why COPPA compliance is the real barrier to AI in schools"
- Product updates: new curriculum uploads, new grade levels

Target: 3 posts/week, 2 long-form articles/month.

**Facebook (B2C parents + homeschool)**

- Join and participate authentically in: homeschool groups, parent education groups, local school parent networks
- Run boosted posts targeting parents of school-age children in target geographies
- No hard-sell in groups — lead with helpful content, mention the product naturally

- Same keyword and creative strategy

### 4.6 Partnerships

**Homeschool Networks**
- Partner with secular homeschool co-ops and curriculum fairs (HSLDA, iHomeschool Network)
- Offer: 3-month free trial for co-op members in exchange for email list access and word-of-mouth
- Cost: Sponsorship of 2–3 homeschool conference booths/year: $500–$2,000 each

**Tutoring Centres**
- Approach independent tutoring centres as resellers: they pay $2/student/month, charge their students $5–8
- Provides recurring B2B revenue with minimal sales effort
- Target: 20–50 tutoring centre partners in Year 1

**Teachers Pay Teachers (TpT)**
- List free sample lesson packs on TpT linking to the app
- TpT has 8M+ teacher users; free resources drive massive traffic
- Cost: Free seller account; time investment to create 5–10 resource packs

**International Schools**
- International schools (IB, Cambridge A-Level, AP curriculum) actively seek multi-language tools
- Target via ISC Research database or direct LinkedIn outreach to school heads
- Offer annual contracts at $5/student/month with a 30-day free pilot

**EdTech Newsletters & Podcasts**
- Sponsored placements in: EdSurge, MindShift (KQED), The EdTech Podcast, Cool Cat Teacher
- Cost: $200–$1,500 per placement depending on audience size

## 5. Launch Campaign — "2am Ready"

A targeted 8-week launch campaign to drive first 500 signups.

**Campaign concept:** "It's 2am. Your exam is in 8 hours. StudyBuddy is ready."

**Why it works:** Exam anxiety is universal and visceral. It positions the app as the always-available, always-patient tutor that no human tutor can match at that hour.

### Week-by-Week Plan

| Week | Activity | Channel | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| -2 | Build waitlist landing page; start collecting emails | SEO + social organic | 200 waitlist emails |
| -1 | Seed TikTok/Reels with "exam prep" content | TikTok, Instagram | 500+ video views |
| **Launch day** | Press release to EdTech media; Product Hunt launch | ProductHunt, PR | 1,000 visitors day 1 |
| 1 | Go live on Google Play; post "launch day" behind-the-scenes | Social, app store | 100 installs |
| 2 | Activate Google Ads (search + retargeting) | Paid search | 300 trial signups |
| 3 | Teacher outreach: 100 personalised LinkedIn DMs | LinkedIn | 10 teacher pilots |
| 4 | First email sequence to waitlist (5-email nurture campaign) | Email | 15% conversion to paid |
| 5–6 | Publish first 4 SEO blog posts | Blog/SEO | Domain authority building |
| 7–8 | First case study: pilot school pass rate data | LinkedIn, email | 3 school demo requests |

### Product Hunt Launch

A strong Product Hunt launch can generate 2,000–5,000 visitors in 24 hours with zero ad spend.

**Preparation:**
- Build hunter network (existing tech/EdTech contacts upvote on launch day)
- Craft gallery: screenshots, a 60-second demo video, clear tagline
- Offer "Product Hunt exclusive": 3 months free for first 100 signups
- Post at 12:01am PST (Tuesday–Thursday performs best)

**Cost:** $0 in platform fees; ~$500–$1,000 in time/creative production.

## 6. Email Marketing

Email is the highest-ROI channel for converting free users to paid and schools to contracts.

### Sequences

**Free user onboarding (B2C):**

| Email | Timing | Subject | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Immediately | "Your first lesson is ready" | First lesson completion |
| 2 | Day 2 | "How did your quiz go?" | First quiz attempt |
| 3 | Day 5 | "You've used 1 of your 2 free lessons" | Urgency before paywall |

**School nurture (B2B):**

| Email | Timing | Subject | Goal |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Immediately | "Your school demo is confirmed" | Show up to demo |
| 2 | Day 3 | "How StudyBuddy works for your curriculum" | Educate on upload flow |

## 12. Quick Wins (Do This Week)

These cost nothing and can be done before the product is publicly live:

1. **Register social handles** — @StudyBuddyOnDemand on TikTok, Instagram, LinkedIn, X. Consistent handle across all.
2. **Set up Google Search Console** — verify the domain; start tracking keyword impressions from day one.
3. **Create a waitlist landing page** — a single-page site with email capture. Mailchimp or ConvertKit free tier. Goal: 200 emails before launch.
4. **Write 3 SEO blog posts** — pick the top 3 searched topics from your grade range (e.g., "Grade 8 math: solving linear equations", "Grade 9 biology: cell division explained"). Publish before launch so they start ranking.
5. **Identify 5 teacher micro-influencers** — search TikTok #TeacherTok and Instagram for STEM teachers with 5K–50K followers. DM them with a free annual access offer in exchange for an honest review post.
6. **Create a Google Business Profile** — even as a digital product, this helps local search visibility for "STEM tutor [city]" searches.
